Honest conversation

On the CPI target

Your math · Jan

$75 LTV ÷ 3 trial-to-install ÷ 30 install rate  =  $0.80 target CPI

The math holds at the model level — if every step of the funnel performs at the rate you've assumed, $0.80 CPI is the answer.

Our published benchmark · Ethan

Meta $3.00–$5.00  ·  TikTok $2.00–$3.50  ·  Apple $2.50–$4.25

The range our paid media team would defend as achievable on a US-market microlearning launch in the first 60 days with this audience set.

Where the gap comes from

The $0.80 number assumes a more efficient install-to-trial conversion than the audience has been showing us in market. Our 3.08% best Meta CTR is excellent — but the funnel from click → install → trial isn't yet conditioned on the kind of demand you'd need for $0.80 CPI to be live. I'm not about to spout off BS here — the $0.80 figure isn't realistic with the data we have today. The Apple Search number ($17 today) is the most honest read we have, and it's above benchmark. Until we restore clean Meta + TikTok attribution and get a stable week-4 read, we'd be guessing about the install rate the $0.80 math depends on.

What we're committing to: revisit the CPI target together at the Week 4 read once attribution is restored. We'll have clean numbers across all three platforms by then, the install rate will be a known, and we can rebuild the model with real data. Until then, $3–5 Meta is the benchmark we're operating against — and the Apple Search number is the truth we're trusting.
